Objective

This unit focuses on how to use basic French grammar and communication skills correctly in the hospitality industry. It helps students form polite, professional sentences while communicating with guests and colleagues.

1. Understanding Gender Forms in French

In French, every noun has a gender — masculine or feminine. This is important because articles, adjectives, and verbs change according to gender.

1.1 Definite Articles (The)

EnglishMasculineFemininePlural
Thelelales

Examples:

  • le menu – the menu
  • la chambre – the room
  • les clients – the guests

If the word begins with a vowel (a, e, i, o, u) or silent h, we use l’ instead of le or la:

  • l’hôtel – the hotel
  • l’eau – the water

1.2 Indefinite Articles (A/An, Some)

EnglishMasculineFemininePlural
A / Anununedes (some)

Examples:

  • un client – a guest (male)
  • une cliente – a guest (female)
  • des serviettes – some napkins

2. Plural Forms

To make a noun plural in French, add “-s” at the end. If it already ends with s, x, or z, do not add anything.

SingularPluralMeaning
le clientles clientsthe guests
la tableles tablesthe tables
l’hôtelles hôtelsthe hotels
le prixles prixthe prices

Pronunciation Tip: In plural forms, the s is usually silent, but in connected speech (liaison), you may hear it:
les‿amis (pronounced lez-ami) – the friends.

3. Structured Writing Exercises

3.1 Writing Guest Notes

Example 1 – Welcome Note:

Bonjour Monsieur/Madame,
Bienvenue à notre hôtel!
Votre chambre est prête au troisième étage.
Le petit déjeuner est servi de 7h à 10h.
Merci et bon séjour!

Translation:
Good morning Sir/Madam,
Welcome to our hotel!
Your room is ready on the third floor.
Breakfast is served from 7 to 10 a.m.
Thank you and have a pleasant stay!

Example 2 – Room Service Note:

Cher client,
Votre dîner sera servi à 20h.
N’hésitez pas à appeler la réception pour toute demande.
Cordialement,
L’équipe du restaurant.

4. French Dining Etiquette

4.1 Basic Dining Rules in France

  1. Keep your hands visible on the table (not in your lap).
  2. Bread is placed directly on the table, not on a plate.
  3. Wait until everyone is served before eating.
  4. Say “Bon appétit!” before starting a meal.
  5. Use formal greetings like Monsieur (Sir) and Madame (Ma’am).
