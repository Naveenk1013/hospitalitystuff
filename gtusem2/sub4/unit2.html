Unit 2: Typology of Tourism

1. Forms of Tourism

a. International Tourism

Example: Imagine Rahul from Mumbai planning a trip to Paris, France. He will need a passport and possibly a visa, and he will experience a different culture and language.

b. Inbound Tourism

Example: A group of tourists from Japan visiting the Taj Mahal in Agra, India. The Indian tourism industry benefits from their visit through foreign exchange and cultural exchange.

c. Outbound Tourism

Example: Rahul decides to travel to Thailand for a vacation. He will contribute to Thailand's tourism industry and experience Thai culture and cuisine.

d. Inter-regional Tourism

Example: Rahul travels from Mumbai in Western India to Kolkata in Eastern India. He will experience different regional cultures, cuisines, and landscapes within India.

e. Intra-regional Tourism

Example: Rahul takes a weekend trip from Mumbai to the nearby hill station of Lonavala. This short trip allows him to explore attractions within the same region.

f. Regional Tourism

The state of Rajasthan promotes its unique cultural heritage and attractions, such as Jaipur's palaces and Jaisalmer's desert safaris, to attract tourists from other parts of India and abroad.

g. Domestic Tourism

Example: Rahul plans a trip to the Golden Temple in Amritsar, Punjab. He travels within India, supporting local businesses and experiencing diverse domestic cultures.

h. Internal Tourism

Example: Rahul visits the historical sites of Hampi in Karnataka. This form of tourism focuses on exploring attractions within one's own country.

i. National Tourism

Example: The Indian government promotes the "Incredible India" campaign to attract both domestic and international tourists to various destinations across the country, fostering national pride and economic growth.

2. Types of Tourism

  • Cultural Tourism: Rahul visits the ancient city of Varanasi to experience its rich cultural heritage and historical sites.
  • Adventure Tourism: Rahul goes trekking in the Himalayas, engaging in physical activities and exploring nature.
  • Ecotourism: Rahul visits the Sundarbans National Park, focusing on conservation efforts and sustainable tourism practices.
  • Medical Tourism: Rahul travels to Chennai for specialized medical treatment, taking advantage of advanced healthcare facilities.
  • Business Tourism: Rahul attends a conference in Bengaluru, combining business meetings with leisure activities.
  • Religious Tourism: Rahul undertakes a pilgrimage to Tirupati to seek spiritual fulfillment.
  • Wellness Tourism: Rahul visits an Ayurvedic retreat in Kerala to focus on health and well being.
  • Sports Tourism: Rahul travels to Mohali to watch an international cricket match, combining his love for sports with travel.

3. Leiper’s Tourism System

Three Main Elements of Leiper’s Tourism System:

  1. The Traveler (Tourist): The person who travels for leisure, business, or other purposes.
  2. Three Key Geographical Regions:
    • Tourist Generating Region (TGR): The place where tourists start their journey (e.g., their hometown).
    • Transit Route Region (TRR): The places tourists pass through while traveling (e.g., airports, highways, or stopover cities).
    • Tourist Destination Region (TDR): The place where tourists go for their vacation or trip.
  3. Tourism Industry (Businesses & Services): Hotels, airlines, travel agencies, restaurants, and attractions that support tourism.

Example of Leiper’s Tourism System:

Imagine a tourist named Rahul from Mumbai planning a trip to Jaipur.

  • TGR (Tourist Generating Region): Mumbai – where Rahul lives and plans his trip.
  • TRR (Transit Route Region): Rahul takes a flight from Mumbai to Jaipur; the airport and airline are part of the transit region.
  • TDR (Tourist Destination Region): Jaipur – where Rahul explores forts, palaces, and local culture.
  • Tourism Industry: The hotel he stays in, the tour guide he hires, and the restaurants he dines at all contribute to the tourism system.

4. Contemporary Trends in Indian Tourism

  • Growth of Domestic Tourism: More Indians like Rahul are exploring destinations within India, such as Goa and Ladakh.
  • Digital Transformation: Rahul uses online platforms to book his trip to Jaipur, including flights, hotels, and local tours.
  • Sustainable Tourism: Rahul chooses eco-friendly accommodations and participates in activities that support local communities and conservation efforts.
  • Wellness and Spiritual Tourism: Rahul visits Rishikesh for a yoga retreat, focusing on spiritual growth and well-being.
  • Adventure and Experiential Travel: Rahul goes paragliding in Bir Billing, seeking unique and thrilling experiences.
  • Medical Tourism: India attracts international patients seeking affordable and high-quality medical treatments.
  • Government Initiatives: Programs like "Swadesh Darshan" promote domestic tourism by developing theme-based tourist circuits.
