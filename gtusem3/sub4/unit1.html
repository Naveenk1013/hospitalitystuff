Lesson 1: Alphabets and Pronunciation

  • Syllabus
  • Home

Objective

By the end of this lesson, students will be able to:

  • Identify all 26 letters of the French alphabet.
  • Pronounce each letter correctly.
  • Recognize differences between English and French sounds.
  • Use correct pronunciation when greeting or spelling guest names.

1. Introduction

French and English share the same 26 alphabets, but the pronunciation is different in many cases.

In hospitality, correct pronunciation is very important — for example:

  • When spelling a guest’s name for a reservation.
  • When reading names, addresses, or room numbers over the phone.

Example:
Receptionist: “Can you spell your name, please?”
Guest: “Oui, c’est M-A-R-I-E — em, ah, air, ee, uh.”

So, let’s learn how to pronounce the French alphabets correctly.

3. Special Notes for Pronunciation

A. Letters that Sound Most Different from English

  1. E (uh) — very short and soft.
    Example: le, de, me. Say it with lips relaxed.
  2. R (air, from throat) — this is a throaty sound, not rolled like in English.
    Example: restaurant, rouge. Try saying it softly from your throat, not the tongue.
  3. U (oo with tight lips) — shape your lips like saying “oo” but say “ee.”
    Example: lune (moon)
  4. G and J — both have a soft “zh” sound (like measure in English).
    • G: gâteau (cake)
    • J: jus (juice)

4. Vowel Sounds in French (Les voyelles)

Vowels are A, E, I, O, U, Y. Each has a pure and clear sound in French, never blended like in English.

Practice Tip: Say the vowels slowly: ah – uh – ee – oh – oo – ee. Repeat twice a day to master pronunciation.

5. Consonant Sounds (Les consonnes)

French consonants are mostly similar to English, except:

  • H is always silent.
    Example: hôtel is pronounced o-tel.
  • Final consonants are often silent unless followed by a vowel.
    • Paris → “Pa-ree” (not “Par-ris”)
    • Blanc → “Blahn” (the ‘c’ is silent)
  • R is always from the throat, not the tongue.

6. Accent Marks in French (Les accents)

Accent marks change pronunciation and sometimes meaning of words. There are five accents in French:

Accent circonflexeêhôtelOften shows a missing “s” in old French

Hospitality Tip: Pronounce “hôtel” as o-tel, not ho-tel. The “h” is silent, and the accent (ˆ) on “ô” makes the sound longer and softer.

9. Classroom Activity Ideas

  1. Listening Practice
    Teacher says a French letter (e.g., “bay”), students write which letter they heard (e.g., “B”).
  2. Name Spelling Game
    Each student spells their name in French aloud using correct pronunciation.
  3. Accent Mark Hunt
    Find 5 French words in your textbook that contain accent marks and write their meanings.

10. Summary

  • French has 26 alphabets, same as English, but different pronunciation.
  • H is silent; R comes from the throat.
  • Accent marks change both sound and meaning.
  • Learning correct pronunciation helps in guest interaction, name spelling, and professional communication.

Hospitality Reminder:
A polite smile and correct pronunciation make your first impression perfect!
In French hospitality, “La politesse et la prononciation sont les clés du succès.” (Politeness and pronunciation are the keys to success.)
