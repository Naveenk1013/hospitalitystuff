Module 4: Hospitality Operations

Hospitality operations form the backbone of guest satisfaction in hotels and other accommodation establishments. The two key operational departments—Front Office and Housekeeping—work together to create a seamless, comfortable, and memorable guest experience.

Efficient coordination between these departments ensures smooth operations, repeat clientele, and a strong brand reputation.

4.1 Front Office Operations

A. Role and Responsibilities of the Front Office

The Front Office is often referred to as the “nerve center” or “face of the hotel.” It is the first and last point of contact for guests and plays a critical role in managing reservations, arrivals, stays, and departures.

1. Role of the Front Office
  • Guest Contact Point: First impressions are formed at the front desk through professional greetings and assistance.
  • Information Hub: Provides information about hotel facilities, local attractions, and transportation.
  • Revenue Center: Generates income through room sales and upselling higher room categories or packages.
  • Coordination Point: Acts as a link between guests and other departments (housekeeping, F&B, maintenance, etc.).
  • Security & Record Keeping: Maintains guest records, room status, and ensures guest safety and confidentiality.
2. Key Responsibilities
Function Responsibilities
Reservation Handling booking requests, maintaining availability charts, confirming bookings, managing cancellations.
Reception Welcoming guests, check-in formalities, verifying identity, assigning rooms.
Cashiering Maintaining guest accounts, posting charges, settling bills.
Information & Concierge Services Providing directions, arranging transportation, handling guest inquiries.
Telecommunication Managing internal/external calls and messages.
Guest Relations Handling complaints, ensuring satisfaction, arranging special requests.
3. Personality Traits of Front Office Staff
  • Politeness and patience
  • Effective communication and grooming
  • Confidence, empathy, and problem-solving ability
  • Attentiveness and time management skills

B. Reservation Systems and Processes

A reservation is an agreement between the guest and the hotel to provide accommodation for a specified period. It helps in forecasting demand, optimizing occupancy, and managing revenue.

1. Types of Reservations
Type Description
Guaranteed Reservation Guest guarantees arrival by advance payment or credit card; room is held even after check-in time.
Non-Guaranteed Reservation Room is held only until a specific time (usually 6:00 PM) unless reconfirmed.
Group Reservation Made for a number of guests under one booking (tour groups, conferences).
Tentative / Waitlisted Reservation Subject to confirmation; guest is informed if space becomes available.
2. Sources of Reservations
  • Direct booking (telephone, email, hotel website)
  • Travel agents and tour operators
  • Corporate or government offices
  • Online travel portals (OTAs) like MakeMyTrip, Booking.com
  • Central reservation systems (CRS) and Global distribution systems (GDS)
3. Reservation Process
  1. Enquiry – Guest asks about room type, rate, and facilities.
  2. Availability Check – Room status verified using PMS or manual chart.
  3. Booking Confirmation – Details recorded and confirmation sent.
  4. Updating System – Reservation entered in the system for future reference.
  5. Pre-arrival Coordination – Communicating guest preferences to concerned departments.
4. Modern Reservation Tools
  • Property Management Systems (PMS): Automates room allocation and updates. (e.g., Opera, eZee FrontDesk)
  • Channel Managers: Synchronize inventory across multiple online portals.
  • Mobile and Cloud-Based Systems: Allow guests to check availability and book instantly.

C. Guest Services and Relations

Guest service is the essence of hospitality, focusing on making every stay comfortable and personalized.

1. Guest Services
  • Luggage handling, wake-up calls, message delivery
  • Airport transfers, valet parking, and travel arrangements
  • Room service coordination
  • Providing local information, maps, and guides
  • Handling lost and found items
2. Guest Relation Activities
  • Greeting VIPs and repeat guests
  • Managing complaints and feedback
  • Organizing special amenities (birthday cakes, flowers, etc.)
  • Conducting post-departure follow-ups through email or calls
3. Importance of Guest Relations
  • Builds guest loyalty and positive brand image
  • Encourages repeat business and word-of-mouth marketing
  • Helps in service recovery after guest complaints
  • Enhances guest satisfaction scores and hotel ratings
4. Use of Technology in Guest Services
  • AI chatbots for instant query handling
  • Digital check-in/check-out systems
  • Mobile keys and room controls
  • CRM (Customer Relationship Management) software for tracking guest preferences

4.2 Housekeeping Operations

A. Role and Responsibilities of Housekeeping

Housekeeping ensures that the hotel environment is clean, safe, aesthetically appealing, and comfortable. It contributes directly to guest satisfaction and retention.

1. Role of Housekeeping
  • Maintain cleanliness in guest rooms and public areas
  • Ensure sufficient supply of linens, amenities, and guestroom supplies
  • Coordinate with front office on room status updates
  • Handle laundry, linen, and uniform management
  • Decorate rooms and public areas for special occasions
2. Organizational Structure
Position Key Responsibilities
Executive Housekeeper Head of department; responsible for planning, budgeting, and supervision.
Assistant Housekeeper Supports the executive housekeeper; handles daily operations.
Floor Supervisor Inspects guest rooms and supervises housekeeping staff.
Room Attendants Clean and prepare guest rooms as per standards.
Public Area Attendants Maintain cleanliness of lobbies, restrooms, corridors, etc.
Linen Room & Laundry Staff Manage inventory of linen, laundry operations, and uniforms.
3. Importance of Housekeeping
  • Ensures hygiene and comfort
  • Promotes a positive guest impression
  • Helps maintain property value
  • Prevents health hazards and accidents

B. Cleaning and Maintenance Procedures

Proper cleaning and maintenance are essential for hygiene, guest comfort, and aesthetic appeal.

1. Cleaning Procedures
(a) Cleaning Agents:
  • Water: Universal cleaning solvent.
  • Detergents: Remove grease and dirt.
  • Disinfectants: Kill germs and bacteria.
  • Abrasives: Used for scrubbing tough stains.
  • Polishes: Add shine to surfaces.
(b) Cleaning Equipment:
  • Manual: Mops, brushes, brooms, dusters.
  • Mechanical: Vacuum cleaners, floor scrubbers, carpet extractors.
(c) Step-by-Step Room Cleaning Procedure:
  1. Enter room (using housekeeping key) and ensure guest is not inside.
  2. Open curtains, switch on lights, and ventilate room.
  3. Collect trash and used linen.
  4. Make the bed with fresh linen.
  5. Clean bathroom (sink, toilet, shower area).
  6. Dust furniture and clean floor.
  7. Replenish amenities (toiletries, stationery, etc.).
  8. Conduct final inspection and report maintenance issues.
2. Maintenance Coordination

Housekeeping coordinates with Engineering & Maintenance to report:

  • Faulty lights or plumbing issues
  • AC or ventilation problems
  • Broken furniture or fittings

Regular preventive maintenance ensures safety and minimizes guest complaints.

C. Inventory Control and Management

Inventory control in housekeeping involves systematic management of supplies, linens, and equipment to avoid shortages, wastage, or overstocking.

1. Types of Housekeeping Inventory
Category Examples
Recycled Inventory Linen, uniforms, guestroom furniture, cleaning equipment.
Non-Recycled Inventory Guest supplies (soaps, shampoos), cleaning agents, stationery.
2. Inventory Control Process
  • Establish Par Levels: Minimum quantity required to support smooth operations.
  • Issuing and Receiving: Proper record-keeping of materials issued or returned.
  • Storage and Labeling: Organized storage to avoid damage or pilferage.
  • Regular Stock Audits: Conducted to verify usage and control wastage.
3. Importance of Inventory Management
  • Prevents stockouts or excess accumulation
  • Controls cost and reduces wastage
  • Ensures consistent service quality
  • Helps in budget forecasting
4. Use of Technology in Housekeeping
  • Computerized Inventory Systems (PMS Integration) to track stock movement.
  • Barcode/RFID Tracking for linen and uniforms.
  • Mobile Apps for maintenance reporting and room status updates.
