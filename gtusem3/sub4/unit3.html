Objective

By the end of this lesson, students will be able to:

  • Use foundational French vocabulary for greetings, time, and directions in hospitality.
  • Understand and apply auxiliary verbs for basic sentence construction.
  • Communicate politely and effectively with French-speaking guests.

1. Importance of Learning French in Hospitality

French is one of the most spoken languages in the tourism and hospitality industry. Many international guests come from French-speaking countries such as France, Belgium, Switzerland, and Canada. Knowing basic French helps hotel employees:

  • Communicate politely and professionally with guests.
  • Enhance guest satisfaction and comfort.
  • Create a welcoming and international atmosphere.

Example: A French guest feels respected and happy when greeted with “Bonjour, Monsieur!” instead of “Hello, Sir!”

4. Grammar Focus: Auxiliary Verbs

Auxiliary verbs are helping verbs that assist in forming sentences. In French, the two main auxiliaries are:

A. ÊTRE (to be)

SubjectVerb FormEnglish Meaning
JesuisI am
TuesYou are
Il / ElleestHe / She is
NoussommesWe are
VousêtesYou are (formal/plural)
Ils / EllessontThey are

Examples:
Je suis réceptionniste. (I am a receptionist.)
Elle est cliente. (She is a guest.)

B. AVOIR (to have)

SubjectVerb FormEnglish Meaning
J’aiI have
TuasYou have
Il / ElleaHe / She has
NousavonsWe have
VousavezYou have (formal/plural)
Ils / EllesontThey have

Examples:
J’ai une réservation. (I have a reservation.)
Nous avons deux chambres libres. (We have two rooms available.)

6. Practice Activity for Students

A. Translation Exercise

Translate into French:

  • Good evening, Madam.
  • Where is the elevator?
  • I have a reservation.
  • Thank you very much.
  • Enjoy your stay.

Answers: Bonsoir Madame, Où est l’ascenseur ?, J’ai une réservation, Merci beaucoup, Profitez de votre séjour.

B. Role Play

Situation: A French guest arrives for check-in.
Student A: Receptionist
Student B: Guest
Practice greeting, confirming reservation, and showing directions to the room.

C. Vocabulary Game Idea

Make flashcards: one side English, one side French. Students take turns guessing the correct translation.
