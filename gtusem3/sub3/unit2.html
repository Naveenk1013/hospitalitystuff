Unit 2: Arrival & Check-In Procedure

In the guest arrival stage there are certain key steps that ensure smooth service and a positive first impression.

Preparing for Guest Arrival

  • Verification of documents
  • Preparing movement list and expected arrival/departure lists

Movement List

It contains the names of guests expected to arrive and depart the next day.

Pre-Arrival Activities

  • Generate an arrivals list with estimated times, room types, and special requirements.
  • Prepare group arrivals, VIP, SPATT (Special Attention Guest), and handicapped guest lists.
  • Distribute relevant lists to Housekeeping, F&B, and Guest Relations departments.
  • Complete pre-registration for guests using information from reservations.
  • Prepare amenities vouchers for guests requesting special items at reservation time.

Preparation at Reception Desk

  • Conduct morning briefing and review the logbook.
  • Calculate expected arrivals and departures.
  • Determine room position:
    Room position = Available vacant rooms + expected departures − expected arrivals.
  • Allocate rooms for VIPs and other guests.

Welcoming or Receiving Guests

First impressions are vital. Reception staff must greet guests promptly, courteously, and with warmth, as this sets the tone for the entire stay.

Why Do Guests Need to Register?

  • Legal requirement for maintaining guest records.
  • Helps account for residents during emergencies.
  • Confirms acceptance of hotel terms and conditions.
  • Engages the guest while booking and room allocation are verified.

Registration Process

After arrival, the front desk agent completes registration to confirm accuracy and gather required details. Guests sign a printed registration card as a statutory requirement. Even with digital systems, a signed card may still be necessary.

Formats Used During Check-In

  1. Guest Registration Card (GRC):

    A vital document containing guest details like name, address, nationality, organization, arrival/departure dates, and room rate.

  2. C Form:

    A legal form filled by all foreign nationals (except Nepalese). Three copies are prepared—one for FRRO, one for the local police station, and one for hotel records.

  3. Arrival and Departure Register:

    Monitors daily arrivals and departures, helping housekeeping and front office track occupancy.

Assigning the Room and Rate

Room assignment involves allocating available rooms in specific categories based on reservation data. Rates and assignments are finalized at registration.

SOP – Concierge / Bell Desk – Guest Luggage Handling Procedure

On Guest Arrival

  • Open the vehicle door, greet the guest, and offer luggage assistance.
  • Retrieve luggage, confirm completeness, and tag it properly.
  • Escort the guest to reception and inform them you’ll handle their luggage.
  • Check PMS for allocated room, write room number on luggage tag, and place it in the room or storage as needed.

On Guest Departure

  • Collect luggage from guest room.
  • Engage courteously and offer taxi assistance.
  • Store or deliver luggage based on guest request and update the movement register.

Luggage Storage Room

  • Keep room organized and divided into arrival, departure, and long-term sections.
  • Ensure all luggage is tagged and recorded in the long-term storage register.
  • Mark fragile or perishable items appropriately and ensure safety and security.

Records and Procedures

1. Left Luggage Procedure

Guests can store luggage safely after check-out. The process includes:

  • Ensure bill settlement before accepting luggage.
  • Check for damage, paste luggage stickers, and issue a signed receipt.
  • Enter details in the left-luggage register and store securely.
  • Release only upon verifying receipt or signature in the register.

2. Scanty Baggage Procedure

Guests with minimal luggage are potential skippers (leave without payment). Controls include:

  • Notify lobby manager and reception on arrival.
  • Stamp “Scanty Baggage” on arrival errand card and registration card copies.
  • Maintain a Scanty Baggage Register signed by the lobby manager.

Topic: Guest History Card

1. Introduction

A Guest History Card (GHC) records essential guest data to personalize service, build loyalty, and support operations.

2. Objectives

  • Maintain past stay records and preferences.
  • Enable personalized experiences and loyalty tracking.
  • Assist marketing and service recovery.

Conclusion

Arrival and check-in are among the most crucial guest touchpoints. Proper documentation, luggage handling, safety measures, and record-keeping create lasting impressions and build guest trust.
