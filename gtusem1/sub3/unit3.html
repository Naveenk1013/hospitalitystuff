1. Introduction

A kitchen is the heart of food service. Safety and quality control prevent accidents, contamination, and wastage while ensuring hygiene and high standards.

2. Kitchen Safety

Precautions to protect staff, equipment, and food from harm.

Importance of Kitchen Safety

  1. Prevents accidents (burns, cuts, slips)
  2. Safe working environment
  3. Protects staff & equipment
  4. Maintains food hygiene
  5. Reduces waste & cost
  6. Increases efficiency
  7. Complies with FSSAI
  8. Builds reputation

Common Kitchen Hazards

TypeExamplesPrevention
PhysicalCuts, burns, slipsUse dry towels, non-slip shoes
ChemicalDetergents, skin irritationStore away, use gloves
BiologicalSalmonella, E. coliTemp control, avoid cross-contamination
FireGas leaks, oil fireCheck lines, keep extinguishers
ElectricalShocks, sparksDry hands, no damaged wires

Safety Rules

  • Wear non-slip shoes, tie hair
  • Check equipment before use
  • Keep extinguishers handy
  • Store raw/cooked separately

6. Fire Safety

Causes

  • Gas leaks
  • Overheated oil
  • Faulty wiring
  • Unattended cooking

Prevention

  • Regular checks
  • Staff training
  • Never leave cooking
  • Clear exits

9. Purchase, Quality & Storage

Five Rights of Purchasing

  • Right Quality, Quantity, Price, Source, Time

Storage Types

  • Dry: 10–20°C (cereals)
  • Refrigerated: 1–4°C (dairy)
  • Freezer: <-18°C (meat)

Quality Standards

  • Meat: Firm, fresh smell
  • Fish: Bright eyes, red gills
  • Vegetables: Crisp, no bruises
  • Eggs: Clean shell, sink in water
